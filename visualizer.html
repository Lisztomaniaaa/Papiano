How does TikTok/Instagram → piano notes work?

Our server briefly downloads the audio from the link and hands it to your browser, where it's transcribed to musical notes using an open-source pitch-detection model (Basic Pitch / TensorFlow.js). The audio isn't stored after your session — transcription itself happens entirely on your own device.

Why is this labeled Beta?

Automatic audio-to-MIDI transcription isn't perfect, especially on full song mixes with vocals, bass, and drums layered together. Notes may sometimes be missed, extra, or timed slightly off compared to a real MIDI file. We're actively tuning this, but it won't sound as clean as a proper MIDI upload.

What's the most accurate way to play a song?

Uploading a real MIDI (.mid/.midi) or MusicXML (.musicxml/.xml) file. Those contain exact note data, so there's no transcription guesswork — what you upload is exactly what plays.

Which platforms and formats are supported?

Paste Link supports TikTok and Instagram video URLs (YouTube is not supported). Upload Video / MP3 accepts .mp4, .webm, .mov, .mp3, .wav, and .m4a files. Upload MIDI / MusicXML accepts .mid, .midi, .musicxml, and .xml files.

Is my file or link stored anywhere?

No. Audio is fetched server-side just long enough to hand it to your browser, and transcription runs client-side — nothing is saved or shared after the page processes it.
